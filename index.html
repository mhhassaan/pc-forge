Quick Tips & Tricks

1

Check Compatibility

Always verify that your CPU socket matches your motherboard before purchasing. Use our compatibility checker to avoid costly mistakes.

2

Plan Your Budget

Allocate 30-40% for GPU, 20-25% for CPU, and 10-15% for storage. Adjust based on your specific use case and performance goals.

3

Power Supply Matters

Don't skimp on the PSU. Choose a reliable 80+ Bronze rated unit at minimum, with 20% headroom above your system's power draw.

4

Thermal Paste Application

Use a small pea-sized amount of thermal paste on the CPU. Too much can actually reduce cooling efficiency. Less is more!

5

Cable Management

Good cable management improves airflow and makes future upgrades easier. Route cables behind the motherboard tray when possible.

6

Test Before Closing

Always do a POST (Power-On Self-Test) with the case open before closing it up. This helps catch issues early.
